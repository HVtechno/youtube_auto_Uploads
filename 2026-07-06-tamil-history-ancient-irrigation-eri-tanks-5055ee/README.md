# Tamil Nadu’s Ancient Water Chain

For centuries, Tamil villages managed rain through linked tanks called eris, turning monsoon bursts into shared water security. This story follows a practical system of engineering, maintenance, and community responsibility that still matters in a hotter, drier world. #AncientWisdom #TamilHistory #WaterManagement #SustainableLiving #ForgottenHistory

**Scene 3**
- Narration: An eri was usually an earthen reservoir with a strong bund, sluices for controlled release, and surplus channels. Many were linked in cascades, so overflow from one tank refilled the next downstream.

**Scene 4**
- Narration: This was remarkable because it matched agriculture to the rhythm of the monsoon. Tanks recharged groundwater, trapped silt for fertile fields, reduced flood damage, and gave villages a shared reason to maintain the commons.
