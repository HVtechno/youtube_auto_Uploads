# The Andean Farms That Survived Frost

Around Lake Titicaca, ancient Andean farmers built raised fields called waru waru to grow food in a harsh highland climate. Their design used water, soil, and temperature in a way modern agriculture is still learning from. #AncientWisdom #AndeanHistory #SustainableFarming #ForgottenTechnology #ClimateResilience

## Scenes

**Scene 1**
- Narration: Long before tractors reached the Andes, farmers near Lake Titicaca built fields that could fight frost. They were called waru waru: raised earth platforms surrounded by water channels.
- Image prompt: Wide shot of ancient Andean farmers standing on long raised earthen fields separated by narrow water canals near Lake Titicaca, high plateau landscape, early morning cold light, historically appropriate woven clothing, no modern objects

**Scene 2**
- Narration: These fields were used by pre-Inca societies in the high plains of present-day Peru and Bolivia, especially around the first millennium CE, where thin air and freezing nights made farming risky.
- Image prompt: Wide aerial-style view of the high Andean altiplano beside Lake Titicaca, clusters of raised agricultural platforms and canals, small pre-Inca village with simple adobe and thatch structures in the distance, late afternoon light

**Scene 3**
- Narration: Farmers dug canals, piled the soil into long planting beds, and grew crops like potatoes and quinoa on top. The canals collected water, provided mud for fertilizer, and helped manage drainage.
- Image prompt: Medium shot of Andean farmers digging a canal with wooden digging sticks and piling dark wet soil onto a long raised planting bed, potato plants visible on the platform, bright midday sun, close view of hands and tools

**Scene 4**
- Narration: The genius was in the water. By day, canals absorbed heat from the sun. At night, they released that warmth slowly, softening frost and protecting crops in one of the world’s toughest farm climates.
- Image prompt: Close-up of a raised waru waru field at night with potato plants on the earthen bed and reflective canal water beside it, faint mist and frost on the ground, moonlit highland setting, low camera angle

**Scene 5**
- Narration: Many waru waru systems were abandoned after conquest, population loss, and later changes in land use. Modern farming often tried to flatten wetlands, forgetting that these older fields worked with water instead of against it.
- Image prompt: Wide shot of partially abandoned ancient raised fields in an Andean wetland, eroded earthen ridges with shallow water channels and wild grasses, cloudy overcast light, no people, distant mountains

**Scene 6**
- Narration: Today, some Andean communities and researchers are restoring waru waru. Remembering them is not nostalgia; it is a practical lesson in farming with climate, water, and local knowledge for the future.
- Image prompt: Medium shot of contemporary Andean farmers in traditional woven hats and ponchos carefully planting seedlings on a restored raised field beside clean water canals, sunrise light, camera framing at human height, no modern logos
